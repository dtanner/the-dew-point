What is this?

I'm building a little watch app that shows a single word for how it feels outside (like Crisp, Muggy, or Raw) based on the temperature and humidity. I want your take on where the lines should be and what to call each zone — there are no wrong answers, it's about how you experience weather.

  1. Edit the bands on the left. Each temperature range has rules that pick a word. Change the cutoffs, rename the words, add or remove zones — make it match how you'd describe the weather.
  2. Watch the grid on the right. It updates live: columns are humidity, rows are temperature, each cell shows the word your rules produce. Hover a cell for details.
  3. Send it back to me. Put your name in the box below, click Download JSON at the bottom right (or Copy to clipboard), and Slack me the file or pasted text.
💡 Rule of thumb: below about 65°F, use Relative Humidity (rh) in your rules — when it's cool, damp air feels raw and dry air feels crisp, and humidity captures that best. At 65°F and above, use Dew Point (dp) — when it's warm, dew point is what makes air feel sticky or muggy. Each band has a “by dp / rh” toggle in its header that picks which one all of that band's rules use, so you only ever think about one number per band. Your edits auto-save in this browser, so you can close the tab and come back.
